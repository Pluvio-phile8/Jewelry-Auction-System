The Process - from Start to Finish

Embarking on your selling journey with F'RANKELLY begins with a valuation and ends with a check in your hand (assuming we are successful!). There are seven simple steps in the process. If you need assistance at any time, don't hesitate to contact us.

Step 1: Preliminary Valuation

Understanding the true market value of your jewelry and/or watches is the first crucial step in your selling journey. The best way to do this is to obtain a "valuation report" from an auction house—preferably one that has an expertise in jewelry and watches, like F'RANKELLY.

Several factors play a part in the valuation process – the item's make and model, condition, age, provenance, quality, current market conditions, and rarity are among the most important factors.

The best way to begin this part of the process (and the process overall) is to fill out our valuation request form. Once F'RANKELLY's team of jewelry and watch specialists receive your request, they will reach out within just hours and either provide you with a valuation report immediately or request some additional information.

Step 2: Shipping

At the conclusion of "Step 1" you will have received a preliminary valuation report as well as a prepaid fully-insured shipping label (for US-based consignors only), a packing slip (to be included inside of the package that you ship to us) which will list the contents of the package as well as the insured value of those contents, and "safe packing instructions".

If you run into any issues or have any questions, reach out for help and we will gladly assist.

Step 3: Final Valuation & Consignment

Once the package arrives at F'RANKELLY's New York City Fifth Avenue office, the team will unpack the items and email you to let you know that we've received the items in good order.

Following this, the team will perform a more-detailed inspection and study of the items and update the valuation report, if needed, and issue you a final valuation report. After you confirm that you are in agreement and would like to move forward, you will be sent a consignment agreement, which, among other things, gives us the legal authorization to represent and sell your jewelry and/or watches.

Step 4: Pre-Auction Preparation

After consignment is complete, our team of jewelry and watch specialists go to work preparing auction catalog descriptions and executing any value enhancement services that might be required (such as obtaining a report from a lab or performing a small repair), all of which would have to be approved by you beforehand and all of which is covered by F'RANKELLY fully upfront.

In addition, our industry-leading photo studio goes to work creating the beautiful content that we are known around the world for.

Step 5: Pre-Auction Marketing

All items in an auction are organized into what are known as "lots". When lots are ready to be presented to our global audience of bidders/buyers, they are uploaded on our online auction platform and many third-party auction sites.

After this, our marketing team begins their work promoting the sale and lots to a global audience.

Interested bidders are allowed to "pre-bid" in any of our auctions, meaning that, for the two weeks (approx.) leading up to an auction, bidders are allowed to bid against one another—our online platform will reflect this bidding activity showing "current bid".

Step 6: Auction Day

On the live auction day, each lot is opened by the auctioneer, bids are accepted from online, absentee, and phone bidders, and winning bidders are declared when the hammer falls. Invoices are sent out to winning bidders immediately following the auction.
